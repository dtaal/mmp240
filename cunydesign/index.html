KCC Student Journal of Arts, “Antheon,” Wins GDUSA 2024 American Graphic Design Award in “Student Work” Category

January 7, 2025
Kingsborough Community College’s student journal of arts, “Antheon,” was named a 2024 American Graphic Design Awards winner in the “Student Work” category by Graphic Design USA (GDUSA). Once again, KCC was the only community college to receive this recognition. Now in its 61st year, GDUSA’s annual showcase reflects graphic design’s power to shape and serve commerce and culture. The award is open to design firms and studios, ad and creative agencies, in-house departments, freelancers, students, and more. They received over 8,000 entries, of which nearly 800 were chosen. In addition to being featured on the GDUSA website, “Antheon” will be included in a printed catalog featuring the winners.

Media Arts and Technology Students Connect with Creative Industry Reps at Internship Event

October 31, 2024
Students, faculty and representatives from New York City’s thriving creative sector filled the 13th floor of the Fiterman Hall conference center for the first-ever Media Arts and Technology (MEA) Internship Discovery Day on October 30. Students learned about internships, apprenticeships and employment opportunities from representatives from creative fields including animation, motion graphics and video arts as well media programming and graphic design. Panelists from Google, Omnicom Group and The One Club for Creativity also spoke. The event offered resume assistance, professional headshots and information about career pathways and other opportunities.
